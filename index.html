Crime on all Levels

Crime on all Levels takes a deep dive into both the global and national statistics surrounding criminal organisations, criminal offences and the drug trade. We focus on three key areas, the first being, how Australia stacks up against the Rest of the World. Are we a safe or dangerous country?

There is also a deep dive on the emergence of cyber-crime and how prevalent it is becoming in modern society for us Australians. Finally, we nail in on the drug trade an undoubtedly debilitating state that is affecting our Australian culture, and an issue that needs to be tackled so as to not continue its upward trajectory.

The Global Scale

The 2023 Global Criminality Index

The Criminality Index is an “assessment of overall organised crime levels in a country.” It is made up of two main components, each of which has several subfactors:

  • Criminal Actors – The structure and influence of different types of criminal organisations.
  • Criminal Markets – Consists of all stages of illicit trade of both commodities or people.

Some of the most dangerous regions around the world currently are Middle Africa and the Middle East. The biggest movers over the last two years have been Maldova and Cambodia, as both countries saw a large uptick in criminal activity.
The Nordic region of the world remains undoubtedly the safest of all, whilst Australia performs quite well with a score of 4.00 in 2023.

2023 Criminality Index vs Resilience Index

The flip side of the Criminality Index is the Resilience Index. Others combined with the factors below measure "a country's ability to withstand and disrupt organised criminal activities" via its own politcal, social, economic and legal landscape.

Australia in that regard has a very formidable societal structure in place with a 2023 score of 7.38, ranking 19th of 193 countries. That places Australia 2nd of the Oceanic countries behind New Zealand, although performing relatively poorly under the Criminality Index compared to the Polynesian countries in the region.

There exists a clear linear relationship between the Criminality Index and the Resilience Index. By far and away Myanmar is one of the most dangerous countries in the world, ranking 1st and 3rd worst in the respective measures.

Cyber Crimes across Australia

Australian's Affected by Types of Cyber-crime


The cyber-crime landscape in Australia has seen a significant increase in the number of offences over the last decade. As technology has continued to improve, there has been a new wave introduction of innovative crimes, where criminals can steal from you all from anywhere in the world.

From 2022-2023 alone the number of offences rose by over 200%. The Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) received an estimated 94,000 reports of cyber-crime across 2023.

The most common type of cyber-crime in Australia was personal fraud and card fraud, which accounts for over 50% of all cyber-crimes committed in the country. These crimes usually involve the theft of personal information, credit card or bank details. More often than not, it is happening right infront of us from clicking spam emails, visiting fake websites or even using public wifi.

Crimes in Victoria and the Drug Trade

Distribution of Criminal Offences in Victoria

Most notably during the 2015-2024 period, the Division that saw the most turbulence was F - Other Offences during 2021-2022. The number of offences rose by over 1900% as public health and safety (F92) breaches skyrocketed during the COVID-19 lockdown period.

Over the last 10 years the drug trade has made up over 6% of the total criminal offences in Victoria. There are now upwards of 30,000 criminal drug offences committed each year in Victoria alone.

Distribution of Drug Crime in Victoria

Which drugs are the most popular?

The vast majority of drug related offences in Victoria stem from the possession of illicit substances. Across the last 10 years drug possession has made up over 80% of the more than 280,000 drug offences in Victoria. The worst of it can be seen at local festivals and events where drug use is rampant and largely uncontrolled.

Like most cities around the world, the predominant drug of choice for Victorians has been Cannabis. Although not yet legalised beyond medical use in Victoria, the easing of Cannabis restrictions in recent years has increased accessibility for those who seek to use it for other means.

Summary

As Australian's we can consider ourselves extremely lucky to be in the position we are in. The Criminality Index and Resilience Index are both strong indicators of the safety and security that we find ourselves in. In times of growing crisis around the world, we can be proud of the culture and society that we have built.

Despite this we should not become complacent, as the drug trade and cyber-crime are clear indications of crime not only on the rise, yet that has also been spilling out of hand in recent times. Tackling these major issues that we face across the country will go along way in ensuring that we continue to be a safe and secure nation.
